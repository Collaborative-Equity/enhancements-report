ARIA - Proposed Developments

Enhancements, Timeframes, Costs & Scope
Date: April 2026

Following on from our meeting at St Pancras on Wednesday, this document covers what's coming next for ARIA - the five enhancements I'm building, how long they'll take, what it costs to run, what we could charge clients, and a few things we'll need to figure out as we go. I am developing five major enhancements that will take the app to the next level.

Part One
The Five Enhancements
What I'm building and what each one will do.

1. Structured Transcript

Right now, when a recording gets transcribed, you get one big block of text. It works, but it's hard to navigate - especially on longer conversations. I'm going to break it into timed, colour-coded sections: coach turns in one colour, coachee turns in another, with timestamps and short labels describing what's being discussed. You'll be able to collapse sections you've already looked at and jump between them from a nav bar at the top.

The really useful bit is linking it back to the critique. When ARIA's critique references a specific moment, that reference will be clickable - it'll jump you straight to the right part of the transcript and highlight the passage. So instead of scrolling through everything trying to find what ARIA's talking about, you just click and you're there.

In practice

This changes how people will actually use the platform day to day. A readable transcript makes the whole critique much more useful because you can see exactly what happened, in context, at the moment ARIA's pointing at.

2. CEQ Knowledge Index

Ask ARIA works well if you already know what you want to ask, but if you're new to the CEQ frameworks - or you've forgotten the detail of something specific - there's no way to browse what's in there. You're just staring at a blank text box.

I'm adding a collapsible panel above the chat input, organised around the four sections of Gareth's manuscript: Coaching, Leadership, Teams, and Change and Strategy. Each topic will have a short plain-English description and an "Ask ARIA about this" button that kicks off a conversation with the right context already loaded. If you don't need it, you collapse it and it's out of the way.

In practice

This means the full breadth of CEQ's intellectual property is visible and browseable, not buried behind a chat interface. New users get a way in. Experienced users who want a refresher on a specific model can find it in seconds.

3. Practice Partner Modes

The Practice Partner currently lets you describe a scenario and practise, which is good, but it treats every conversation the same way. In reality, coaching someone feels completely different to having a difficult conversation about performance, or talking to someone who's anxious about a restructure.

I'm adding six modes: Coaching (what we've got now), Difficult Conversation, Accountability, Performance Conversation, Change Conversation, and Leading Under Pressure. Each one will change how ARIA behaves - the character it plays, the kind of pressure it puts on, and which CEQ frameworks it's drawing on behind the scenes. At the end of a session, ARIA will give a short debrief - a few paragraphs on how you handled it against the relevant frameworks.

In practice

This is where it starts to feel like a proper practice environment. Managers don't just coach - they have tough conversations, they hold people to account, they lead through uncertainty. Giving them somewhere safe to rehearse each of those, with ARIA behaving differently each time, is a big step up.

4. Session Preparation

This will be a new pill in Ask ARIA alongside Coaching, Practice Partner, Ask ARIA and Question Generator. The idea is simple: you've got a meeting coming up - a one-to-one, a team huddle, an all-hands, whatever it is. You tell ARIA who it's with and what it's about, pick the meeting type, and ARIA produces a preparation pack: a timed agenda, briefing notes in CEQ language, targeted coaching questions, and personal watch-outs.

It'll link both ways with the Question Generator too. If you've already generated some questions, you can build a full prep pack from those. If you start with a prep pack, you can ask for more questions tailored to that session.

In practice

This is the one that gets ARIA into people's daily routine. It's not just about learning after the fact - it's helping a manager walk into their next conversation with the right questions and the right mindset already in place.

5. Critique Lenses

At the moment, every recording gets the same critique: GROW stage analysis, OUCH! habit tracking, qualitative feedback and speaking time. That's useful as a general overview, but it doesn't let you focus on what you actually want to work on.

I'm adding eight lenses, each one focusing on a different CEQ framework: Overall (what we've got now), OUCH!, GROW/GROW+, 7 Coaching Triggers, SuperListening, Logical Levels, BreakThrough, and the full CEQ Methodology. You'll pick a primary lens and optionally add a secondary one. The basics - speaking time, summary, strengths, development areas and recommendations - will always be there regardless of which lens you choose.

Quick note on Modes vs Lenses: These are deliberately separate things. Modes are in Practice Partner - they change how ARIA behaves during a role-play. Lenses are in the Record Portal Critique - they change how ARIA analyses a real recording afterwards. I'm keeping that distinction clean throughout.
In practice

If you're working on your listening, you pick the SuperListening lens and get specific feedback on whether you're using the coachee's own language, how you're using silence, and how well you're picking up on what's being said beneath the surface. ARIA will also flag the areas it can't assess from audio alone - things like body language, eye contact and other non-verbal cues - as a reminder to the user to stay aware of those in the room. If you're prepping for a BreakThrough session, you can see exactly how your coaching lined up with that model. The critique becomes personal rather than generic.

Part Two
Build Timeline
The order I'll build them in and how long each phase should take.

I've ordered these by risk and impact. The first two are the quickest changes with the least chance of breaking anything. The lenses come last because they need the most testing.

PhaseEnhancementEstimated Effort
Phase 1Structured Transcript (chunked, speaker-coded, navigable)3 - 5 days
Phase 1CEQ Knowledge Index1 week
Phase 2Practice Partner Modes (framework + first 2 modes, then remaining 4)2 weeks
Phase 3Session Preparation (5th pill + all meeting types)2 weeks
Phase 4Critique Lenses (starting with OUCH! and SuperListening, through to all 8)4 - 5 weeks

Total: roughly 6 - 8 weeks for everything, working through in order. Phase 1 should be done within a fortnight of starting.

Order for developments: The Structured Transcript and Knowledge Index are both display changes - I'm not touching the AI engine, so there's very little that can go wrong. Practice Modes and Session Preparation build on top of what's already there. The Critique Lenses are the most involved because each one needs its own prompt, and each prompt needs proper testing against real recordings to make sure the output is accurate and useful.

No disruption to what we've already got

None of this will affect what's already built. Everything we currently demo stays exactly as it is - the new features will be added on top, one at a time, so we'll be able to show progress as we go. I'll aim to get as much as possible finished before the demo with David.

Part Three
What It Costs to Run
Platform running costs, excluding my development time.

ARIA runs on Google Apps Script as a web app, so there are no server or hosting bills - it all sits on Google's infrastructure through a standard Workspace subscription. The only real ongoing costs are the two AI services, and they're both pay-as-you-go.

AI subscriptions (development tools)

The development work uses two AI subscriptions. Claude Max is the main one - I'm currently on the Max 5x plan at $100/month (roughly £80), but I'm already hitting the usage ceiling regularly during heavy development periods, so I may need to move up to the Max 20x plan at $200/month (roughly £160). I also use a ChatGPT Plus subscription at $20/month for additional development and testing work. These are fixed monthly costs that cover the development and AI tooling side, not the platform's own API calls.

Part Five
The Bigger Picture
Beyond the five enhancements and what still needs working out.

Gareth's ARIA pitch document sets out a longer-term ambition that goes beyond these five enhancements - a platform that doesn't just teach coaching frameworks but weaves them into how a whole organisation operates. The company's own principles, their people processes, right down to the individual manager preparing for their next one-to-one.

That's a four-layer model: CEQ IP, company principles, people processes, individual manager. The five enhancements cover the first layer properly and start to touch the second. The deeper layers are where the real customisation happens, and that depends on things we can't fully plan for until we're working with an actual client.

What I can build now

Everything in Part One. All five enhancements use the technology and architecture we've already got. No new APIs, no new infrastructure, nothing we haven't done before. The 6-8 week estimate is based on the actual work involved.

What'll depend on the client

The pitch document describes a platform that's bespoke to each organisation - coaching frameworks mapped to their values, their competency models, their performance processes. That's all doable, but how much work it is will vary hugely from client to client. Some organisations have their competency frameworks well documented and we could load them fairly quickly. Others will need to work through that thinking first, with CEQ's help, before we can build it in.

That's not a problem - it's just how it works. But it does mean we can't put a timeline on the deeper customisation until we know who we're working with and what they've already got in place.

Things we'll need to figure out

Areas for consideration:

Needs scoping
360-Degree Feedback

The pitch document includes 360 feedback as part of the platform. There's a big difference between ARIA analysing someone's own recordings (which we can do now) and ARIA collecting and pulling together feedback from their peers, reports and line manager. That second bit involves data collection, anonymity, report design and governance questions that need proper thinking through before we can commit to a timeline.

Needs scoping
Individual Manager Profiles

The fourth layer of the model is the individual - ARIA knowing someone's habits, development areas and progress over time. Some of that will come naturally as critique data builds up. A proper individual profile system where ARIA adjusts its coaching based on everything it knows about that person is a bigger piece of work that'll need its own spec.

Ongoing consideration
Data Governance

As we collect more data - transcripts, critiques, practice sessions, prep packs - the governance questions get more important. Who owns the data? Who can see whose results? How long do we keep it? What happens when someone leaves? We've got GDPR signposting and an AI policy declaration in place already, but the governance framework will need to grow as the platform does.

Ongoing consideration
Scaling Up

ARIA's currently set up for cohort-based programmes. If we move towards full organisational deployment with hundreds of managers using it at once, there'll be questions about user management, reporting dashboards for L&D teams, and how it fits alongside existing HR systems. On the technical side, we may also need to migrate from Google Apps Script to Google Cloud Run at that point to handle the extra load and keep things running quickly. All solvable, but worth thinking through properly with each client.

The five enhancements are scoped and I'm ready to build them. The bigger vision is all doable, but the deeper stuff will take longer and we'll need a real client involved to make the right decisions. Better to be upfront about that than overpromise.
Part Six
Resources & Dependencies
What's needed to get this done.

Development

I'll build all five within the existing Google Apps Script and web app setup, using AI-assisted development the same way I built what we've got now. No additional dev resource needed for the five enhancements. If we wanted to tackle the deeper customisation work (360, profiles, full org rollout) at the same time, that'd be a different conversation, but for this lot we're fine.

CEQ team input

A few of these will need someone from the CEQ team to review specific bits: the Knowledge Index descriptions (making sure the plain-English summaries of each framework are right), the Practice Partner mode behaviours (do they feel true to the frameworks?), and the Critique Lens outputs (is the analysis accurate and useful?). Not a huge time commitment, but it needs to happen at the right points.

Testing

The biggest quality risk is in the Critique Lenses. Each lens is basically a prompt that tells the AI how to analyse a recording against a specific framework. If the prompt isn't right, the output won't be right either. Each one needs testing against real coaching recordings before it goes live - that's why I've put the lenses last in the build order and allowed time for iteration.

The other four enhancements are lower risk. The transcript is just a display change on data we already have. The Knowledge Index reads from a data file. Practice Modes and Session Preparation are variations on things that already work.

Summary
Where We Are

ARIA's in a good place. It's already a proper working product, not a prototype. These five enhancements will make the critique more flexible, give users a way to browse and explore the full CEQ knowledge base, create a realistic practice space for the kinds of conversations managers actually have, and help them prepare properly before they walk into a meeting.

The bigger vision - bespoke customisation per organisation, 360 feedback, individual profiles - we'll get there, but it makes sense to do it with a real client rather than guessing at what they need. For now, five enhancements, 6-8 weeks, and the running costs are pennies.
